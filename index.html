About him

Nelson Mandela became the first black president of South Africa in 1994, serving until 1999. A symbol of global peacemaking, he won the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993.

  • Nelson Mandela was born on July 18, 1918, in Mvezo, Transkei, South Africa.
  • Becoming actively involved in the anti-apartheid movement in his 20s, Mandela joined the African National Congress in 1942.
  • For 20 years, he directed a campaign of peaceful, nonviolent defiance against the South African government and its racist policies.
  • In 1993, Mandela and South African President F.W. de Klerk were jointly awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for their efforts to dismantle the country's apartheid system.
  • In 1994, Mandela was inaugurated as South Africa's first black president.
  • In 2009, Mandela's birthday (July 18) was declared "Mandela Day" to promote global peace and celebrate the South African leader's legacy.
  • Mandela died at his home in Johannesburg on December 5, 2013, at age 95.
